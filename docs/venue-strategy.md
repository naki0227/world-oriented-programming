# Venue Strategy Draft

## Recommendation

The best current provisional target is:

1. `Onward! Papers` or `Onward! Essays` at SPLASH

Secondary targets:

2. `LIVE`
3. `VL/HCC`
4. `SLE`

## Why Onward! Fits Best Right Now

The current project is strongest as:

- a new programming paradigm proposal
- a research vision with an executable prototype
- a paper that combines philosophy, language design, and initial validation

This aligns closely with Onward!'s self-description as a venue for grand visions, new paradigms, and ideas that are well argued even when they are not yet validated at the level expected in more conventional PL venues.

The current draft already has the structure that Onward! tends to reward:

- a strong problem framing
- a clear conceptual thesis
- a prototype that demonstrates feasibility
- a broad research direction beyond the immediate artifact

## Why The Others Are Secondary

### LIVE

LIVE is attractive because the project ultimately aims at a more immediate and cognitively aligned programming experience.
It is a particularly good future venue once the diagrammatic or interactive side becomes stronger.
Right now, however, the work is still more about the paradigm and runtime model than about live interaction itself.

### VL/HCC

VL/HCC is a plausible target once the human-centered and visual interface story is stronger.
The current prototype already points in that direction, but the present paper is still more language-model and paradigm oriented than evaluation-heavy or interface-centered.

### SLE

SLE fits the language-design and implementation side of the project well.
However, the current manuscript is more visionary and representational than engineering-heavy.
If the next phase produces a more mature DSL, implementation methodology, and language-tooling story, SLE could become a stronger fit.

## Practical Submission Strategy

### Phase A

Treat the current paper as an `Onward!`-style submission.
Optimize for:

- clarity of the paradigm claim
- conceptual originality
- tight connection between philosophy and executable prototype
- honest presentation of what is and is not yet validated

### Phase B

After the paper is in stronger shape, resume implementation and push on one of these two directions:

1. interactive visual environment
2. stronger empirical or comparative evaluation

### Phase C

Use that next generation of the work for:

- `VL/HCC` if the visual-human-centered side becomes central
- `SLE` if the language-engineering side becomes central
- `LIVE` if immediacy and live interaction become central
