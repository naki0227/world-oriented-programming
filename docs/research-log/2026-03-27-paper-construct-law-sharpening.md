# 2026-03-27: Paper Construct-Law Sharpening

The seed paper already had the core semantics and visibility material in place, but its central claim still read slightly more like a general paradigm shift than a specific computational thesis.

This pass sharpened the manuscript around a more explicit center:

- programs construct worlds,
- laws state admissible development, and
- execution is the evolution of those worlds under law.

The edits were intentionally narrow.
They focused on the abstract, introduction, and evaluation framing rather than on adding new technical content.
The goal was to make the paper's main contrast more legible:
\emph{sekai} keeps world construction and world law explicit, whereas imperative baselines tend to bury both inside update procedures and guard logic.

This should make the seed paper read less like a broad paradigm sketch and more like an executable claim about representation and execution.
